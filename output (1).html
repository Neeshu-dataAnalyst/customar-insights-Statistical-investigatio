Step 5: Formulate Hypotheses (10 Points)

Business Purpose: Turn business questions into statistical tests.

Confidence Level (): For all tests, we use a significance level of $\alpha = 0.05$.

Hypothesis 1: Gender vs. Monthly Spend (Independent t-test)

  • Business Question: Do males and females spend differently?
  • Null Hypothesis ($\text{H}_0$): The average monthly spend is the same for Male and Female customers ($\mu_{Male} = \mu_{Female}$).
  • Alternate Hypothesis ($\text{H}_a$): The average monthly spend is different for Male and Female customers ($\mu_{Male} \neq \mu_{Female}$).

Hypothesis 2: Education vs. Monthly Spend (One-way ANOVA)

  • Business Question: Does education level impact average monthly spend?
  • Null Hypothesis ($\text{H}_0$): All Education Levels have the same average monthly spend ($\mu_{High School} = \mu_{PhD} = ...$ ).
  • Alternate Hypothesis ($\text{H}_a$): At least one Education Level group has a different average monthly spend.

Hypothesis 3: Marital Status vs. Number of Pets (Chi-square test)

  • Business Question: Is marital status related to the number of pets owned?
  • Null Hypothesis ($\text{H}_0$): Marital Status and Number of Pets are independent (not related).
  • Alternate Hypothesis ($\text{H}_a$): Marital Status and Number of Pets are related.

Hypothesis 4: Age vs. Customer Activity (Correlation)

  • Business Question: Are older people less active? (Age vs. DaysSinceLastInteraction)
  • Null Hypothesis ($\text{H}_0$): There is no correlation between Age and Days Since Last Interaction ($\rho = 0$).
  • Alternate Hypothesis ($\text{H}_a$): There is a correlation between Age and Days Since Last Interaction ($\rho \neq 0$).

Hypothesis 5: State vs. Monthly Spend (ANOVA)

  • Business Question: Does state-wise spend vary significantly?
  • Null Hypothesis ($\text{H}_0$): The average monthly spend is the same across all States.
  • Alternate Hypothesis ($\text{H}_a$): At least one State has a different average monthly spend.

1. Gender vs. Spend Independent t-test 0.735 Fail to Reject Gender does not significantly impact Monthly Spend.
2. Education vs. Spend One-way ANOVA 0.922 Fail to Reject Education Level does not significantly impact Monthly Spend.
3. Married vs. NumPets Chi-square Test 0.228 Fail to Reject Marital Status and Number of Pets are not statistically related.

Step 7: Present Business Insights

The statistical investigation showed that all five Null Hypotheses failed to be rejected, meaning demographic factors do not statistically influence spending or activity.

Key Recommendations:

  1. Uniform National Strategy: Implement a unified, national marketing strategy as spending patterns do not differ significantly by Gender, Education, or State (Tests 1, 2, 5). This saves costs on complex segmentation.
  2. Focus on Behavioral Data: Shift personalization efforts away from static demographics (Age, Gender) to transactional factors (what, when, how much they buy) to drive higher engagement.
  3. Target Inactive Customers: Implement a strong customer win-back program targeting those with very high DaysSinceLastInteraction (high recency) to prevent churn.
  4. Pet Segment Opportunity: Develop dedicated product lines and promotions for customers based purely on their Number of Pets, as this is an independent segment not related to marital status (Test 3).
